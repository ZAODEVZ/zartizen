# Community-fund playbook - how the best funds run, and how ZAO copies them

How successful communities run + fill their Artizen funds, distilled into a model for the ZAO Fund(s).
From research/846, /847, the fund directory, and the live fund renders. Snapshot 2026-06-13.

## The pattern (what every good community fund does)

A community turns its identity into a fund, fills it with its OWN people's projects, cross-lists those
projects into adjacent funds, and rallies its members to buy + boost. The fund is a multiplier on a
crowd that already exists.

## Exemplars

### Greenpill Fund for Regenerative Gatherings (the closest model to ZAO Festivals)
- Run by the **Greenpill Network**. Filled it with its own chapters + regen-aligned gatherings.
- Roster (~18 in competition): Greenpill chapters (Cape Town, London Ontario, NYC, Ukraine, Uganda,
  Kenya, Nigeria, Cote d'Ivoire), AgroforestDAO, Rifai Sicilia DAO, The Living Village, Vida Verde, etc.
- #1 in the fund: **Edge Esmeralda** (also #2 in the ZAO fund) - proof that one project lives in two
  funds and stacks the match.
- Lesson for ZAO: a fund is "your community's gatherings, branded into one pool, cross-listed." ZAO does
  this for music/artist festivals.

### Console Fund for Community Builders
- Run by Console (a Web3 community platform), sponsor-backed (~$20K pool).
- Bar to qualify a community: **50+ members, 15+ active over 4 weeks.** ZAO at 188 members is ~4x.
- Winners (Naoba Music +2.2 ETH, Zato +2.91 ETH) came from a community-platform fund rallying its people.
- Lesson: the buyer/voter crowd is the whole game; ZAO already has it.

### Funding the Commons Frontier Fund (#1 fund at snapshot, $51K)
- Run by Funding the Commons; commons/research/cultural work. Tied to a real IRL event (Frontier Tower).
- Lesson: pairing a fund with a flagship IRL moment pulls attention + sales (ZAOstock does this for ZAO).

### Terminus Fund (Artizen-run, network states)
- Funds sovereign-community experiments (Grandmatopia, Ipê Village, Agartha). $100K seed.
- Lesson: Artizen actively backs the "community materializes IRL" thesis - ZAO is squarely in it.

## How to FILL a fund (recruit projects)

1. Define eligibility crisply so creators self-identify (the /festivals page + fund proposal do this).
2. Open call to your own community first - the cohort seeds the fund (WaveWarZ, SongJam, ZAOstock, etc.).
3. Recruit aligned outside organizers - the white-space pitch ("the only fund for X, run by people who do X").
4. Tell every project to curate into ALL funds they fit - stacking is the incentive.
5. Rally votes during curation (top ~30% advance) - ZAO's 188 members are the lever.

## How to FILL the match pool (recruit sponsors)

- Start with the $50K Artizen seed (community-fund program) + a ZAO-treasury Founding Sponsor stake.
- Recruit brand sponsors: music/event brands (Restream, Magnetiq, labels, festival-infra), Web3 orgs
  wanting cultural credibility. Pitch: "your dollars become community-directed match; you don't pick
  winners, the crowd does" (kit/sponsor-onepager.md).
- 100% of sponsor dollars go to the fund (10% top-project prize, 90% even match); Artizen takes 0%.

## How a fund CLIMBS the leaderboard

- Sales unlock match; boosts + sales together drive the Boost Score that ranks projects.
- A fund's standing reshuffles every drive (the ZAO fund went #2 -> #4 in a week). Consistent weekly
  rallies beat one big push.
- A flagship project that sells (Edge Esmeralda, InfiniteZero) carries the whole fund's visibility.

## The ZAO model (synthesis)

Run two funds (Emerging Culture + Festivals), fill them from the ZAO portfolio + aligned outsiders,
cross-list every project into adjacent funds, rally the 188 members weekly, and pair the Festivals fund
with ZAOstock/Zaoville as the flagship IRL moments. The edge is the crowd + the consistency, not capital.
