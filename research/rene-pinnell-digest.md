# René Pinnell digest - thesis, roadmap, commitments

A standalone reference on Artizen's founder so the team can track whether he hits his targets. Distilled
from his newsletter (news.artizen.fund) and research/845. Quotes are load-bearing - kept verbatim.

## The thesis

Markets are "good at pricing exclusion, terrible at pricing shared value." Creators drive civilization
yet stay poor. **Artifacts let markets *see* cultural impact** - "just as money let markets see beyond
barter, and stocks let them see scale." Artizen is capitalism finally able to fund creativity, via an
endowment flywheel meant to fund human creativity forever.

> "Why do we fail to fund creators? ... most creators are poor. Rage at this cruel irony fueled me."

## The flywheel (how it's meant to compound)

ART token (a Revnet on Ethereum mainnet) -> a cut of every transaction feeds the Endowment -> the
Endowment funds creators -> creators sell Artifacts -> revenue funds ART buybacks -> repeat.

> "The more ART we sell, the bigger the Endowment grows. The bigger the Endowment, the more creators we
> fund. The more creators we fund, the more Artifacts they sell ... and the cycle repeats."

Buy split: 60% ART to buyers, 10% team, 30% Endowment. Mint price doubles on a schedule (35d -> 120d ->
annual). Redeem ART for USDC at a rising floor, minus a 10% exit tax.

## The targets (track these)

- Endowment launched **$4M** (Oct 2025), **+$360K** in two weeks. As of Jun 2026: **~$14M** (past the $10M-EOY pace).
- **$10M by end of 2026.**
- "In less than five years ... **$10 billion**, overtaking Kickstarter as the largest funding platform for creators."
- "By 2034 ... pass Novo Nordisk to become the largest endowment in the world."
- "Around year twelve ... **$1 trillion.**"
- Assumes 161%/season growth decaying to 2%/season. He frames it as a bet with stated odds: "Everest is
  littered with the frozen remains of big dreams."

Near-term reality check to watch: the $4M -> $10M -> the live $14M trajectory. If it stalls or the ART
floor comes under pressure, the flywheel is reversing (the reflexivity risk).

## The reflexivity risk (the honest downside)

The loop runs backward too: if belief/inflow slows, secondary ART price drifts to the USDC floor, the
10% exit tax means late redeemers recover less, and Endowment growth stalls. René's safeguard claim:
"It's belief - anchored to reality" - the floor is locked USDC, so it is NOT a Terra/LUNA zero. The
realistic bear case is "growth story breaks, value converges to floor." No public stress test exists.
**Keep ZAO treasury out of the ART curve until there is one.**

## The curation rubric (use it)

**Weird, Wonderful, Working.** Weird = original, unclaimed territory ("a feature, not a bug"). Wonderful
= beauty + purpose. Working = ships, sells, shows "signs of life." Curate ZAO submissions to this bar.

## Track record (mostly verifiable from his own archive)

- 5th-generation artist. Childhood best friend was **Ross Ulbricht** (Silk Road) - the root of his
  "peaceful exit, not revolution" worldview.
- Prior ventures: Hurricane Party; Kaleidoscope Events (VR festivals, Tribeca/Cannes deals, "a financial
  black hole"); the **Black Realities Grant** (Viola Davis, $100K, 2020 - his viral breakout).
- **Stripe killed a $20K-MRR business over chargebacks -> $437K personal debt -> bankruptcy.** The
  literal "Phoenix." He puts failure front-and-center as his credibility marker.
- Raised ~$1.2M pre-seed (Protocol VC, Animoca, ConsenSys, Juan Benet, Path); accelerators Tachyon +
  Techstars (mentors incl. Sam Williams, Arweave). Rebuilt ~10x on Bubble with solo dev Nate Van Cleve.
  (A $2.2M figure - Decrypt 2023 - and $2.5M - 2025 - also appear; likely pre-seed -> seed -> restated.)

## Worldview / what he wants from communities

"I am an artist, not an entrepreneur. I trust intuition more than metrics ... I want to dance." Embraces
failure + experimentation; building **network-state** infrastructure (the Terminus Fund). Wants
community funds run by "leaders rooted in creative communities." Met Kevin Owocki (Gitcoin) in 2018, who
invested - "same tribe, different instruments"; they co-run ETH Boulder.
